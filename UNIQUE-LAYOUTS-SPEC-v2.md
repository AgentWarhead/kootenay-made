# Unique Layout Spec v2 — Psychology-Refined, 12/10 Industry Designs

## Design Philosophy
Every demo page must answer the business owner's REAL question: **"Will this make my phone ring / get me bookings / bring people through my door?"** Not "does this look nice."

### Psychology Principles Applied Per Industry:
- **Loss Aversion** → Show what they're missing (competitors getting their customers)
- **Social Proof** → Industry-specific trust signals (not generic "Licensed & Insured")
- **Anchoring** → Lead with the most impressive stat/number
- **Endowment Effect** → Make them mentally "try on" the design as theirs
- **Hick's Law** → One clear CTA per section, not competing buttons
- **Goal-Gradient** → Show progress/journey visuals to pull them forward

## 1. AUTOMOTIVE (Iron Horse Garage)
**Owner persona:** Mike, 52. Owns a 4-bay shop in Trail. Gets most business from word of mouth. Has a Facebook page, no website. His competitor "Precision Auto" just got a slick site and is stealing Google traffic.

**What Mike actually needs to see:**
- That the website will get him found on Google (not just look cool)
- That it shows off his WORK (custom builds, restorations — his pride)
- That it handles the phone call funnel (someone Googles "mechanic near me Trail BC" → lands on site → calls)

**Refined layout:**
- **Hero:** Full-bleed dark. Chrome gradient text. ONE stat that matters: "Trail's Most-Reviewed Shop — 4.9★ on Google" (anchoring). Emergency number visible. Single CTA: "Book Your Service"
- **"The Lift" showcase** (replaces gallery): Staggered before/after vehicle cards — each card flips on hover/tap to reveal the transformation. NOT side-by-side. Think card-flip reveal. 3 projects max (Hick's Law).
- **Services: Diagnostic-style checklist** — styled like an actual shop inspection form. Checkboxes, service names, price ranges. Feels tangible, not abstract.
- **"Currently in the Bay"** section: 3 vehicles with progress bars (10%, 65%, 90%) — creates urgency and proves they're busy (social proof through implied demand)
- **Trust strip:** ASE Certified badge + "25+ Years" + "All Makes & Models" + Google review stars — horizontal, not stacked
- **Testimonials:** Single auto-scrolling ticker (race-stripe style) — fast, bold, continuous. Not pause-and-read cards.
- **Contact: Split — left side form, right side Google Maps embed + hours**

**Unique differentiator:** The card-flip vehicle showcase. No other demo has interactive card reveals.

## 2. TRADES-INDUSTRIAL (Kootenay Pro Plumbing)
**Owner persona:** Dave, 44. Licensed journeyman. Runs a 3-truck operation. Loses emergency calls to "the other guys" because he doesn't show up on Google at 2AM. His wife manages the Facebook page.

**What Dave needs to see:**
- SPEED. Emergency response is his business. The site must scream "we answer fast"
- Service area — people need to know he comes to THEIR town
- That he looks bigger/more professional than a one-man operation

**Refined layout:**
- **Hero: "We Answer" split-layout** — left: bold headline + subline. Right: MASSIVE phone number with a pulsing "AVAILABLE NOW" dot (like a live status indicator). This is THE differentiator for trades. Nobody does the live-status phone number.
- **"Response Zone" map** (replaces gallery): Interactive-style SVG map of West Kootenay region with pins on towns served. Each pin has a tooltip: "Trail — avg response: 22 min". Demonstrates coverage AND speed.
- **Services: Emergency-first grid** — top row is a full-width red/orange "EMERGENCY" card (burst pipe, no heat, gas leak). Below: standard service cards in a 2x3 grid. Visual hierarchy screams "we handle emergencies."
- **"Last 5 Jobs" ticker** — scrolling strip: "✓ Burst pipe — Rossland — 35 min response" / "✓ Water heater — Trail — same day". Real-time feel (social proof + urgency).
- **Trust:** "Licensed • Bonded • Insured" + WorkSafeBC + 500+ jobs badge
- **Testimonials:** Star rating + job type tag + response time mentioned. "Called at 11pm, Dave was here by midnight. Lifesaver." — format that emphasizes SPEED.
- **Sticky emergency bar:** MORE prominent than the regular sticky bar. Red bg, phone number, "Emergency? Call Now" — appears after scrolling past the hero.

**Unique differentiator:** The live-status phone number with "AVAILABLE NOW" indicator + response zone map.

## 3. FARM-HARVEST (Valley Roots Farm)
**Owner persona:** Sarah, 38. Runs a 5-acre organic farm with her partner. Sells at the Saturday market and through word of mouth. Wants CSA subscriptions and online ordering. Seasonal income is a challenge.

**What Sarah needs to see:**
- That the site can actually SELL (not just look pretty) — online ordering, CSA signups
- Seasonal flexibility — the site adapts to what's growing NOW
- Community connection — farms are personal, the site should feel personal

**Refined layout:**
- **Hero: "What's Fresh This Week" banner** — the hero IS the product. Not a generic welcome. Shows 3-4 items currently available with simple illustrations or colored circles. Changes seasonally. This is the **Zeigarnik Effect** — an incomplete list makes you want to see more.
- **"The Harvest Box" CTA** (replaces services): Single prominent card showing this week's CSA box contents with a "Subscribe" button and price. ONE clear action (Hick's Law). Below: "How It Works" 3-step illustrated flow.
- **Seasonal market grid** (replaces gallery): Items organized under season tabs (Spring / Summer / Fall). Each item is a small card with name, growing note, and availability status (🟢 Available / 🟡 Coming Soon / 🔴 Season Over). Farmers market stall aesthetic.
- **"From Seed to Table" story strip** — horizontal scroll showing the journey: Soil → Seedling → Growing → Harvest → Your Table. Hand-illustrated style. (Endowment Effect — they emotionally invest in the process)
- **Recipe of the Week** — single card with ingredients from this week's harvest. Personal touch that no template offers.
- **Community board** — upcoming market dates, farm events, volunteer days
- **Testimonials:** Handwritten-font quotes on kraft-paper textured cards. "The tomatoes taste like my grandmother's garden." — emotional, not transactional.

**Unique differentiator:** "What's Fresh This Week" hero that IS the product + seasonal market tabs with availability status.

**Unique differentiator:** The trip search bar in the hero + conditions widget. Nobody else has a booking-flow feel.

## 5. CLEAN-PROFESSIONAL (Ridgeline Financial)
**Owner persona:** Karen, 48. CPA with 15 years experience. Just left a big firm to start her own practice. Needs to look established from day one. Her clients are high-net-worth individuals and small business owners.

**What Karen needs to see:**
- AUTHORITY. The site must communicate "we know what we're doing" in 3 seconds
- Trust — financial services clients are risk-averse. Every detail matters.
- Clear segmentation — she serves different client types who need different things

**Refined layout:**
- **Hero: Asymmetric authority layout** — large headline left ("Your Money Deserves a Plan"), credential badges stacked right (CPA • CFP • 15 Years • 200+ Clients). Dark navy bg. NOT centered — asymmetry communicates confidence. Single CTA: "Book a Consultation"
- **"Who We Serve" tabs** (replaces generic services): 3 tabs — Individuals / Business Owners / Retirees. Each tab reveals a different set of services and a tailored value prop. This is **segmentation psychology** — people convert when they feel the message is FOR THEM specifically.
- **Case study cards** (replaces gallery): 3 cards — "Challenge → Approach → Result". E.g., "Small business losing $40K/year in tax inefficiencies → Restructured as S-Corp → Saved $38K in year one." Numbers sell (Anchoring).

- **Trust architecture:** Credentials section with actual certification badges (not just text). "Why Ridgeline" section with 3 differentiators (not 6 — Paradox of Choice).
- **Testimonials:** Formal. Name + title + company. "Karen restructured our books and saved us from an audit. Worth every penny." — B2B style, not consumer-casual.
- **Contact: "Schedule a Confidential Consultation"** — the word "confidential" matters in financial services. Calendar-style booking widget.

**Unique differentiator:** "Who We Serve" segmented tabs + case study cards with dollar amounts.

**Owner persona:** Dr. Patel, 39. Dentist. Bought a practice 3 years ago. Patients are aging out, needs to attract young families. His current site is a Wix template his receptionist made. He's losing new patients to the clinic down the road that has online booking.

**What Dr. Patel needs to see:**
- ONLINE BOOKING. This is the #1 thing — patients choose dentists based on booking convenience
- Anxiety reduction — most people fear the dentist. The site must feel calming, not clinical.
- New patient funnel — first visit should feel easy and frictionless

**Refined layout:**
- **Hero: Calming centered layout.** Headline: "Nervous? We Get It." Subline: "Gentle care for the whole family — now booking online." Prominent "Book Online" button. Soft teal gradient. LOTS of white space. The design itself should reduce anxiety.
- **"Your First Visit" journey** (NEW — no other demo has this): 3-step visual path with icons: 1. Book Online (no phone call) → 2. Fill Forms at Home (downloadable) → 3. Meet Your Team (we go slow). This demolishes the #1 barrier: "what happens when I get there?"

- **"Meet Dr. Patel" section** — photo + warm bio + credentials. Patients choose PEOPLE, not practices. This is **Unity Principle** — create connection before the visit.
- **Insurance & payment** badges row: "We Accept: Pacific Blue Cross, Sun Life, Direct Billing Available." This removes a huge friction point.
- **"Smile Gallery" before/after** — teeth transformations (the most compelling gallery type for dental — but using styled placeholder boxes, not real photos)
- **Testimonials:** "Patient Stories" — first name + treatment: "I hadn't been to a dentist in 8 years. Dr. Patel made it painless. Literally." Anxiety-reduction through peer stories.
- **Contact: Online booking CTA + map + "Running Late?" text/call option**

**Unique differentiator:** "Your First Visit" anxiety-reduction journey + treatment icons (no photos).
